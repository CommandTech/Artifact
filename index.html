About

This website contains various articles about the use of AI in the news and the ethical implications that follow, along with some information about issues such as hallucination and a lack of data traceability which invalidate the credibility of LLMs. A few AI-generated articles were also included with a guide on how to identify AI usage and/or content generated by AI with common indicators. The indicator number of each one present per article are in the color gradient; in the article text, yellow highlight was added in specific places where the indicator is present. Note that not all easily-identifiable signs of AI were highlighted, as we encourage you to try and spot them yourselves! The subject matter of these articles is purposely goofy/conspiratorial, as publishing convincing articles, even if within context of the project, goes against the principle (of decreasing AI-fueled misinformation) that we are trying to argue.

    Indicators of AI-generated Content

    Ordered from most to least incriminating:

    1. Notes intended for the user: "Let me know if you'd like it edited further!", "Certainly. Here is a draft of ..."
    2. Empty links and fake sources: "Figure 1: Genius Institute"
    3. Use of emojis when not requested: "👾 Most likely cause of the bug:"
    4. Excessive use of em dashes: "That is spot on—and honestly pretty funny"
    5. Overuse of boldface: "developed a <bold>reinforcement learning (RL) model</bold> to ..."
    6. Prepositional use of to: "From exploring blank to discovering blank, ...
    7. Emphasis on subject matter: "blank stands as a symbol", "blank plays a significant role"
    8. Heavy use of conjunctions: "Furthermore, ...", "Whether you enjoy blank or drawn to blank"
    9. Focus on challenges: "Despite its success, blank faces challenges"
    10. 'Rule of three' listings: "blank contains elements of art, nature, and culture"
    11. Editorializing/introducing its own opinions: "It is important to consider", "It is worth noting"

    Note: The presence of any of these indicators is not a guarantee of AI-generated content. This list only intends to document some patterns commonly displayed by LLMs. This list is not exhaustive; as new models emerge, their indicators evolve with them. This list was developed with inspiration from the Wikipedia article "Signs of AI writing", which can be found here.
